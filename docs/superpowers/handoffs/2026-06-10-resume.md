### IMPORTANT correction made 2026-06-10 (read this before trusting older notes)

The first version of the report (and the 2026-06-05 memory) attributed жқҫжөҰ's attractor effect to a **`hitCount` tie-breaker / high embedding norm**. **That mechanism is wrong for Manual mode:**
- `EmbeddingBasedSpeakerTracker.loadProfiles` resets every loaded profile's `hitCount` to **1**, and `setSuppressLearning(true)` freezes it вҶ’ the `hitCount` tie-breaker (`identify`, lines ~128-132) is **inert**.
- Cosine similarity is **scale-invariant** вҶ’ жқҫжөҰ's higher embedding norm (0.610) **cannot** affect ranking.

The corrected mechanism: жқҫжөҰ wins because it is the **session's dominant talker** вҖ” long/most turns make it the persistent `lastConfirmedId` + the Viterbi-favored state, and it fills most rolling windows. The leading lever is **rolling-window granularity** (15 s window / 7 s chunk swallows short interjections by others), compounded by **Viterbi stay-bias (0.9)**. The empirical counts are unaffected; only the mechanism changed. **Do NOT spend effort on the `hitCount` tie-breaker вҖ” it does nothing in Manual mode.**
