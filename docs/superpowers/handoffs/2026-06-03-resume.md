## Why the next step is NOT A++ (0.95)

The 0.9 fix targeted *stochastic* within-speaker wobble. The residual flips are *structural*: two specific pre-registered profiles are persistently close in 256-d embedding space. Viterbi will lock onto the more probable one regardless of who's actually speaking — no stay-probability tuning solves this. Going 0.95 would *mask* legitimate speaker transitions without resolving the confusion.
